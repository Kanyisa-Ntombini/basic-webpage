Historical Places

Boksburg Cultural Village

  • This village teaches tourists and the people in the community about the different types of cultures of South Africa
  • Visitors get to eat traditional food, be entertained by traditional dance and music, and sleep in authentic dwellings
  • The villages are owned and run by local communities and help uplift the marginalised community out of poverty

Tambo Memorial Hospital

  • It was established on the 17th of August 1905 with 70 patient patient beds and named Boksburg-Benoni Hospital. This has grown to 640 beds and about 1100 staff members.
  • In 1946 it conducted the first corneal transplant in South Africa and in 1953 it conducted the first pupilary implant in the world
  • On the 1st of April 1997 Boksburg-Benoni Hospital's name change to the Tambo Memorial Hospital in honour of the late Oliver Tambo

Chris Hani Museum

  • The house of anti-apartheid activist, Chris Hani, where he was gunned down in his driveway on the 10th of April 1993, has been turned into a museum to depict his life
  • This museum will be connected to a liberation trail that connects to the Thomas Nkobi Cemetery
  • Chris Hani was leader of the South African Communist Party (SACP) and chief of staff of Umkhonto we Sizwe, the armed wing of the African National Congress
